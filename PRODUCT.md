# Product

## Register

product

## Users

UX OS is a personal workflow workspace for a UI/UX designer managing client projects from brief through handoff and case-study writing. The primary user is the owner working inside active project decisions, not a general SaaS audience or marketing visitor.

## Product Purpose

The product keeps UX project work organized across phases: dashboard triage, kanban status, contacts, phase notes, uploaded artifacts, testing findings, handoff notes, and AI-assisted case-study generation. Success means the next useful action is obvious, captured work stays connected, and the workspace feels pleasant enough to use daily.

## Brand Personality

Light, tactile, focused, playful, and personal with a cold paper-and-rain color mood inspired by Konan: pale paper, midnight ink, mist blue, rain blue, animated paper motion, and restrained burgundy.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic dark AI-dashboard styling, purple-blue gradient templates, lifeless bordered card grids, decorative charts, tiny low-contrast labels, and buttons that feel static or disconnected from the action they trigger.

## Design Principles

- Make the next design action visible before secondary options.
- Preserve familiar product patterns while adding playful tactile feedback.
- Use light surfaces, clear grouping, and strong text contrast to reduce friction.
- Use the Konan-inspired palette as atmosphere, not decoration: cold blues for structure and focus, paper whites for work areas, burgundy only for priority or destructive emphasis.
- Keep palette and component changes aligned with `DESIGN.md` so future edits do not drift into generic dashboard colors.
- Use motion more freely than a normal client SaaS product because this is a personal workspace, but keep the motion tied to feedback, state, and anime-paper atmosphere.
- Treat empty, loading, error, hover, focus, and active states as part of the product, not afterthoughts.
- Keep the information architecture stable so existing workflows continue to work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible contrast, visible keyboard focus, touch targets of at least 44px for important controls, semantic form labels, non-color-only status, and reduced-motion fallbacks for transitions.
